ERP System Improvement

Design consulting and platform enhancement

Enoterra — a wine distributor operating in Poland and across Europe — reached out for product design support to evaluate and refine their ERP system, which was struggling with usability and workflow issues.

Role
UX Designer · Design consultant
Client
Enoterra · Wine distribution
Status
Implemented and expanding
Method
Workshops

  2. 2 Audit the main flows to review UX/UI patterns and accessibility
  3. 3 Lead user tests and prioritize changes
  4. 4 Create AI prototypes
  5. 5 Prepare and present a full handover package

UX challenge

The ERP was originally built with AI tools, without design input — which left unclear workflows and recurring usability issues. The challenge was to run focused research to surface those gaps and turn them into clear opportunities. That meant talking to end-users, defining real requirements, running user tests and stress-testing the core workflows.

Workshop method

Participants
5 — Account Manager, Sales Manager, System Administrator, Director, Owner
Tools
FigJam for collaborative mapping; one-on-one interviews for in-depth insights; group workshop for shared alignment and co-creation

Empathy mapping & key personas

User research mapped the team's needs, pain points and the roles that shape daily operations.

Account Manager

Goals Manage client accounts & orders; track account profitability.

Pain Slow search; hard to find old orders.

Feelings Frustrated when the system is slow; workarounds add stress.

Opportunities Faster search; saved views per client; AI assistant to surface delayed orders.

Sales Manager

Goals Analyze sales; understand which products sell.

Pain Limited filters; hard to see trends.

Feelings Frustrated when stuck.

Opportunities Better filtering; sales performance overview; AI assistant to spot expiring stock & trends.

System Administrator

Goals System development & maintenance; adding new features.

Pain Unclear error messages; lack of summary.

Feelings Frustrated by ad-hoc requests; stretched thin during absence.

Opportunities Integration with warehouse system; operational dashboard with alerts.

Director

Goals Monitor operations; get a high-level overview.

Pain Lack of reports; lack of advanced filters; hard to track stock value & forecasting.

Feelings Cautious — needs more visibility.

Opportunities KPI dashboard; trend reports; single dashboard as the daily entry point.

Owner

Goals Trust the numbers; have a clear financial picture.

Pain Lack of financial reports; lack of trend analytics.

Feelings Wants confidence in the data before decisions.

Opportunities Easy access to financial data; cross-period comparison; AI summary of key business signals.

Using Factory AI (Droid and Claude) agents made it possible to build functional prototypes for rapid hypothesis testing and problem-solving. This approach helped clear technical hurdles early through collaborative teamwork, validating interactions, integrations and AI behaviour directly in code.

Prototyped features

Operational Dashboard

New home screen with KPIs, trends, notifications, AI alerts and quick actions.

AI Assistant (Enoterra AI)

Embedded across all views — surfaces delayed orders, low stock and expiring products, with inline diffs and one-click revert.

Context-aware

The assistant adapts its suggestions and actions to the view you're currently working in, so the help feels relevant instead of generic.

Command Palette (⌘K)

Context-aware overlay for navigation, quick actions and AI queries from a single search field.

Validation

We tested the prototype directly with the whole team — the warehouse operator, sales lead, account manager, system administrator, director and owner — so every role had a chance to try the new flows in their own context. They walked through real tasks, comparing the new experience against the old workflow to see where things actually got faster or simpler. We also stress-tested the AI on tricky edge cases (delayed orders, low stock, expiring products) to make sure its suggestions stayed predictable and trustworthy.

What we learned from each round was folded back into the design, so by the time the project was handed over, it already reflected real feedback — not assumptions.

Sneak peek the prototype

Walkthrough of the operational dashboard, Enoterra AI assistant, and ⌘K quick search — navigation, actions and AI queries from one field.

The handover was structured so the client team could confidently take over the redesigned product. It combined three formats:

  • Product showcase — a walkthrough of the new version, framing each key flow (dashboard, AI assistant, ⌘K, orders and warehouse) against the original problem and user feedback.
  • Functional workshop — a hands-on session where the team explored real scenarios and AI interactions in the prototype together.
  • Reference documentation — a deck and operator manual covering interaction patterns, edge cases and AI logic, so the team can onboard new members on its own.

Outcome

Unified workspace

One operator, one screen, one source of truth for KPIs, orders, stock and notifications.

Trusted automation

AI decisions are visible, reversible and never silent: every action lands in a log with inline diffs and one-click revert.

Fewer hand-offs

Synced ERP/WMS removes dual-entry; automated reporting and scoped external access keep partners updated without exposing the network.

Faster operations

Fewer tools, fewer clicks, fewer authorized access points.

Lessons Learned

This project reminded me that even the smartest system stops being useful if people don't know how — or why — to use it. The original product had real capabilities, but no clear UX foundation, and that gap quickly became visible once we sat down with users and the client.

A structured audit and a few honest conversations with operators uncovered friction that no feature list would have surfaced on its own. AI on top of unclear flows only multiplies confusion, so my main focus as a designer was to slow down, listen, and shape the experience around how the team actually works — not how the system was built.
