Local Run Club App

Shaped by the community

The Local Run Club App motivates runners through challenges, local events,
and community. It connects athletic effort with everyday life — turning runs into rewards, local offers, and partnerships with neighborhood businesses. The project is developed
with athletes from Hyrox clubs Dvor and Athletic House in Warsaw, and with support from local businesses in Żoliborz, Warsaw.

UX challenge

With limited initial data, direct engagement with the community became the key source of insight. Conversations and surveys with athletes and race participants — supported by prototype testing and input from local small businesses — helped shape the core requirements for a loyalty program that feels clear, motivating, and valuable for both users and partners.

Health & well-being

Progress & achievement

Community & belonging

Rewards & recognition

Need Tangible and experiential rewards (discounts, spa, free entries).

Self-image & confidence

Need Feeling attractive and proud of oneself.

Pain Boredom or loss of motivation over time.

Routine & balance

Need Work-life balance, getting outside, mental rest.

Pain Sitting too long at work, repetitive lifestyle.

Key personas

The Dedicated Runner · Intermediate to pro

Goal Stay motivated and engaged between races.

Needs Challenges that keep training exciting and rewards for consistent effort — like a free coffee after a long run.

The Newcomer · Beginner

Goal Build a running habit and gain confidence.

Needs A simple, welcoming experience with clear progress tracking, small milestones and friendly competition with similar-level runners.

The Local Business Owner · Partner

Goal Attract new customers and boost local visibility.

Needs A simple way to join the loyalty program — offering perks that bring runners through the door and grow community presence.

The Cashier · Staff

Goal Provide quick and seamless service.

Needs An easy-to-use system to verify and redeem rewards — keeping the line moving and customers happy.

Visual communication

The visual part focuses on an intuitive and recognizable sports theme, real photos and user-generated content. Emphasis is on transparency in the loyalty program rather than active marketing.

Community values and visual identity · Leaderboard case

One of the debated points was the approach to competition in designing leaderboards and similar features. Collected data showed that participants have an average motivation to compete with others.

Step 03 · Trade-offs

Variant A · Personal list

Less competitive Reduces the pressure of direct competition.

Less separation Minimizes the feeling of being at the bottom of a list.

Community Participants feel part of a group rather than in a race.

Own progress Encourages users to track personal growth.

Variant B · Leaderboard

Social approval Uses public recognition as a primary driver.

Clear leaders Stronger division between leaders and the rest.

Rivals Frames other users as competitors to beat.

Competition Uses the thrill of competition to increase engagement.

After considering several approaches to visualizing leaderboards and challenge participants, it was decided to focus members on personal progress rather than competing against others. Consequently, when joining a challenge, participants cannot see others' results until they have joined. Additionally, leaderboards have moved away from podiums and overtly highlighting the top-performing participants. However, elements like medals and leaderboard rankings were kept to preserve a sense of competition.

Lessons Learned: Turning Community Insights into Impact

This project reinforced the importance of staying close to end users. Even without extensive analytics, meaningful insights can be gained through direct communication and observation. Combining quantitative data with hands-on testing proved essential in shaping a product that truly reflects user needs. The final app was designed under strong community influence — built not just for the users, but with them.
