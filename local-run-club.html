Local Run Club App

Shaped by the community

A mobile loyalty concept for runners in Warsaw — challenges, local events, and rewards from neighborhood businesses. Co-designed with athletes from Hyrox clubs Dvor and Athletic House and with local business owners in Żoliborz. Research and prototyping are complete; launch is on hold.

UX challenge

With little existing data, the community became the main source of truth. Surveys and interviews with runners, plus conversations with local businesses, defined what a loyalty experience should offer — and what partners would realistically support.

Personas

The Dedicated Runner · Intermediate to advanced

Goal Stay engaged between races.

Needs Meaningful challenges and rewards that match effort — e.g. a free coffee after a long run.

The Newcomer · Beginner

Goal Build a habit and confidence.

Needs Simple onboarding, clear milestones, and low-pressure comparison with peers at a similar level.

The Local Business Owner · Partner

Goal Bring new customers through the door.

Needs A light way to join the program — perks that are easy to honor and worth offering.

The Cashier · Staff

Goal Keep checkout fast.

Needs Quick reward verification — no friction at the counter.

Customer journey map

01 · Awareness

Action Hears about the club or sees a local campaign.

02 · Onboarding

Action Signs up and links activity tracking.

03 · Engagement

Action Logs runs, joins challenges, checks progress.

04 · Rewards

Action Browses perks and local offers.

Need Rewards that feel worth the effort.

Pain Thin or generic catalog.

Opportunity Mix everyday perks (coffee, gear) with experiences (spa, races).

05 · Redemption

Action Redeems points or sends them to a friend.

Need Recognition without hassle.

06 · Retention

Action Reviews progress and sets new goals.

Need Reasons to return after the first reward.

Pain Motivation drops once novelty wears off.

Opportunity Seasonal challenges and streak rewards.

Visual direction

Sport-native visuals, real photography, and user-generated content. The loyalty layer stays transparent — progress and perks first, not promotional noise.

Leaderboard decision

Competition split the group: some wanted public rankings, others preferred private progress. Research and sketches tested how much social pressure the product should apply.

Step 03 · Trade-offs

Variant A · Personal list

Lower pressure Less fear of ranking last.

Inclusive Group feel over head-to-head rivalry.

Self-focus Progress framed against personal goals.

Variant B · Leaderboard

Recognition Status visible to the group.

Clear hierarchy Leaders stand out immediately.

Rivalry Others become competitors to beat.

Variant A aligned better with mixed competition appetite and beginner needs. The prototype hides other participants’ results until you join a challenge, avoids podium-style leaderboards, and keeps lighter cues — medals and rank — for those who enjoy friendly competition.

Findings & changes

Active coupons

Active coupons moved to the loyalty card sheet — one tap closer at redemption.

Shortened navigation paths

Fewer steps to activities and key menu items from the home screen.

Warning message

Confirmation warns that sending points affects your status — tested users missed this without it.

Tappable points balance

Click maps showed users tapping the balance expecting navigation — it now opens the Points tab.

What stayed with me

An early concept, paused before launch — but the process still matters. With limited analytics, direct conversations and prototype tests surfaced what runners and partners actually needed: local rewards worth earning, redemption without friction, and competition that does not push beginners away. The direction was shaped with the community, not only for it.
